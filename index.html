Renee O'Byrn, UX Designer

I am a UX Designer and Researcher based out of Minneapolis, MN. I have a deep passion for learning and solving person-centered problems with an empathetic lens. I thrive off of solving complex problems and am driven to develop creative solutions with accessibility and inclusivity in mind.

My Skills

User Research

My user research skills include collecting qualitative data through creating interview plans and conducting interviews and collecting quantitative data by creating and conducting surveys distributed to users. Additional user research skills include creating researched-based user personas, empathy maps, user journey maps, affinity diagrams, storyboarding, and usability testing.

Data Analysis

I am skilled at analyzing quantitive and qualitative data including website analytics that are used to ideate solutions to human-centered problems.

User Interface Design

My interface design skills include creating low-fidelity mock-ups and wireframes, mid-fidedlity and high-fidelity wireframes, conducting herustic analysis, accessibility testing, card sorting, sitemapping, and atomic design.

My Work

CAIR MN Website Redesign

Through user research and the design thinking process, our team redesigned the CAIR (Council on American Islamic Relations) MN website to create a user-centered responsive web design. My role included uer research, ideation, wireframing, style guide, prototyping, testing, and iteration.

App Development Project: Candor

Our team developed an app that would support and guide users through the steps of preparing for difficult conversations using an evidence-based framework called Dialectical Behavior Therapy. My role included user research, definition and ideation, wireframing, prototyping, testing, and iteration.

Federal Student Aid Website Redesign

Based on user feedback and research, our team created a user-centered redesign of the Federal Student Aid website. My role included creating user paths, heuristic analysis, usability testing, wire framing, redlining, card sorting, wireframing, prototyping, testing, and iteration.
